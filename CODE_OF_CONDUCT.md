# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

We are committed to building Rodrigo as a privacy-first, ethically designed platform. This commitment extends to how we build it — contributors are expected to uphold these values in every decision, discussion, and line of code.

---

## Privacy & Online Safety Principles

These principles are non-negotiable and apply to all contributors, maintainers, and community members.

**Privacy first.** User privacy is not a feature — it is a foundation. Every contribution that touches user data, content, or behaviour must default to the most privacy-protective approach. Collect the minimum necessary, retain it for the shortest time necessary, and never use it for purposes beyond what the user has consented to.

**No surveillance or tracking.** We do not build hidden tracking, behavioural profiling, or any mechanism designed to monitor users without their explicit, informed knowledge. Contributions that introduce such mechanisms will not be accepted.

**Online safety.** Rodrigo is a campus platform used by students. Contributors must take online safety seriously. Features must not enable harassment, stalking, doxxing, or the non-consensual sharing of private information. Safety tooling — reporting, blocking, moderation — is treated as critical infrastructure.

**Ethical design.** We do not build dark patterns, manipulative engagement mechanics, or interfaces designed to exploit psychological vulnerabilities. Design and engineering decisions should serve users, not extract from them.

**Transparency and honesty.** Be honest in code, in documentation, and in community interactions. If a feature has privacy implications, say so clearly. If you are unsure, ask.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes
- Focusing on what is best not just for us as individuals, but for the overall community
- Raising privacy or safety concerns early and openly, without fear of dismissal
- Reviewing contributions with user wellbeing in mind, not just technical correctness

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information, including real names, addresses, or contact details, without their explicit permission (doxxing)
- Building or proposing features that undermine user privacy, enable surveillance, or introduce dark patterns
- Dismissing or minimising legitimate privacy or safety concerns raised by others
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct — including contributions that violate the privacy and online safety principles above.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, and also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the community leaders responsible for enforcement via the contact listed in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md). All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.
